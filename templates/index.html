Fertilizers in India

Fertilizers are essential for enhancing soil fertility and crop productivity. Below is detailed information about common fertilizers used in India:

  • Urea: Contains 46% Nitrogen. Used for crops like rice, maize, and banana. Application rate: 50-150 kg/ha.
  • Super Phosphate: Provides 16% Phosphorus. Ideal for rice, chickpea, and banana. Application rate: 40-60 kg/ha.
  • Potash (MOP): Supplies 60% Potassium. Used for pomegranate, grapes, and coconut. Application rate: 30-150 kg/ha.
  • DAP: Contains 18% Nitrogen and 46% Phosphorus. Suitable for pigeon peas, watermelon, and orange. Application rate: 50-70 kg/ha.
  • Ammonium Nitrate: Offers 34% Nitrogen. Used for maize and kidney beans. Application rate: 40-60 kg/ha.

Standards: Fertilizer application must align with soil testing and crop-specific needs.

Our Prediction Algorithms

AgroPredict leverages cutting-edge machine learning to recommend crops based on soil and climate data (N, P, K, temperature, humidity, pH, rainfall).

  • Decision Tree: Splits data based on feature thresholds (e.g., rainfall > 100 cm).
  • Logistic Regression: Predicts probabilities for crop suitability.
  • Random Forest: Averages predictions for higher accuracy.
  • Naive Bayes: Assumes feature independence, quick for small datasets.
  • SVM: Finds the optimal hyperplane for high-dimensional data.
  • XGBoost: Optimizes prediction accuracy through gradient boosting.

How It Works: Models are trained on historical crop data and predict the best crop for your input.
